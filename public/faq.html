Some Questions worth asking

How Dangerous is UV-C Germicidal Cleaning.

Any exposure will cause signicant eye damage and may cause skin damage. Do not look into UV light source. Wear UV eye and skin protection. Access panels and warning signs must be in place during appliance operation

What is UV-C Light?

UV-C (short-wave) from 100 to 280 nm or as it's called in the industry -Ultraviolet germicidal irradiation (UVGI) is a disinfection method that uses short-wavelength ultraviolet C (UVC) light to inactivate microorganisms by destroying nucleic acids and disrupting their DNA, leaving them unable to perform vital replication proccesses.

How is UVGI better than chemicals?

The germicidal effects of the UV-C radiation destroy the DNA of Bacteria, Viruses, Spores, Fungi, Moulds and Mites avoiding their growth and proliferation. UVGI technology is a physic disinfection method with a great costs/benefits ratio. It is ecological and unlike chemicals it works against every microorganisms without creating any resistance. Multiple organisations and boards with a global reach, such as WHO, EPA, CDC, ASHRAE and many others, recommend using UV-C rays to disinfect water, environments and air-conditioning systems.

Is UVGI cleaning cost effective?

UV-C radiation devices can be installed into most environments and machinery and can be programmed to maintain the same level of disinfection day and night, ensuring ideal conditions of hygiene without any fluctuation in quality. GermTECH's UV-C-equipped devices offer low running costs and are relatively maintenance-free, besides normal periodical lamp replacement. These powerful and highly durable systems provide exceptional value for money. For this reason, eliminating germs using UV-C as opposed to (or together with) other systems offer excellent results at a low cost.

Does UVGI kill the corona-virus?

At this time there are no specific tests available on UV resistance of the Coronavirus SARS-Cov-2, but there are examples of scientific literature about the UV-C treatment of very similar viruses, such as SARS or MERS which are 99%bsimilar in RNA structure.
